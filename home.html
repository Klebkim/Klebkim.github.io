AC/DC (Power)

The difference between AC and DC is the way current flows. AC flows back and forth and DC flows straight. Examples of AC in real life could be washing machine, refrigerator, and air conditioners. Some real life examples for DC might be a cell phone, TV, and computers

CPU

CPU stands for central Processing Unitis the main part of the computer and I like to call it the most active one. Their task is to computate around. They also have a fan to keep it cool since it might burn.

RAM

RAM stands for Random Access Memory and by RAM it means short term memory such as browsing the internet, editing spreadsheet, and such.

Motherboard

Motherboard is called a motherboard because it is a mother of the board. It connects all the things needed such as memmory, hardware, and bunch of wires.

Storage

There are two different storages called Solid State Drive and Hard drive. Hard drive is cheaper and has more space but Solid State Drive is faster, lighter, more durable, and less energy.
